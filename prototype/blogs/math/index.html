Let A and B be two sets. For any set X , there exists a set Y and functions f : X → A , g : X → B , f' : Y → A , g' : Y → B , and h : X → Y such that:
  • h is unique up to isomorphism,
  • f' and g' are compatible with f and g via h ,
  • f = f' ⋅ h and g = g' ⋅ h .
In other words, for any set X , we can find another set Y that satisfies certain conditions, and functions f , g , f' , g' , and h that form a commutative diagram. The existence of these functions and the commutativity of the diagram are consequences of the universal property of product.
